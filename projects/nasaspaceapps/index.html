NASA SpaceApps Hackathon 2024

Brief Description

This is the first international hackathon that we are participating from TMCL. A group of 5 middle school students - Arishta Jain, Pranav Shah, Shaili Pramanik, Tanay Periwal, Vivaan Saraf and mentored by - Vinay Ummadi. The SpaceApps Challenge is an annual flagship hackathon organized by NASA and other space agencies around the world. The event is a 48-hour global hackathon that encourages collaboration and innovation to solve challenges relevant to both space exploration and social needs. The event is open to anyone who is interested in space and technology, including students, engineers, artists, and entrepreneurs. Participants work in teams to develop innovative solutions to challenges in areas such as space exploration, earth observation. As a end result teams will have to develop/propose software/hardware solutions to the problem statement.

Challenge 2024

Our team TAVSP Tacklers has chosoen the Create Your Own Challenge category. That means we have to define our own problem statement and solve for it.

Problem Identified

After a lot of brainstorming we have identified that decline of fresh water resources is a major problem in the world. There are many reasons for the decline of fresh water resources. Some of major reasons are Climate Change, Population Growth, Urbanization, Industrialization, Deforestation, Overuse of water resources etc.

Now the problem is how we monitor and act on these ready to die and critically import water fresh water resources.

The problem has been identified by @Arishta Jain and @Pranav Shah after a lot of research and brainstorming.
