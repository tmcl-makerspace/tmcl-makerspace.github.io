Challenge 2024

Our team TAVSP Tacklers has chosoen the Create Your Own Challenge category. That means we have to define our own problem statement and solve for it.

Problem Identified

After a lot of brainstorming we have identified that decline of fresh water resources is a major problem in the world. There are many reasons for the decline of fresh water resources. Some of major reasons are Climate Change, Population Growth, Urbanization, Industrialization, Deforestation, Overuse of water resources etc.

The below images show a images shows drying up of fresh water resources in different parts of the world. If you see in difference in the images between first row (image taken from LANDSAT 1984) and second row (image taken from LANDSAT 2021 and 2022) you can see the difference in the water levels/water area cover in the lakes.

Why This Problem is Important?
  • Critical for Life: Freshwater is essential for drinking, agriculture, sanitation, and sustaining ecosystems. The loss of freshwater lakes threatens biodiversity and human survival.
  • Impact on Communities: Many communities rely on lakes for drinking water, fishing, and agriculture. The depletion of these resources can lead to displacement, food shortages, and economic hardship.
  • Climate Change Indicator: Shrinking lakes serve as an early warning sign of the effects of climate change, reflecting changes in precipitation, temperature, and land use.
  • Water Scarcity Crisis: With global populations rising, the demand for freshwater is increasing. Depleting lakes exacerbates the growing global water scarcity crisis.
  • Environmental and Economic Consequences: The decline of freshwater lakes disrupts ecosystems, leading to loss of biodiversity and increased risks of natural disasters like droughts and floods.

Our Solution

What's Been Developed So Far

We have developed a Python script that performs the following tasks:

  1. Satellite Image Retrieval: By providing the latitude and longitude of a specific water body (or any location), the script interacts with the Google Earth Engine API to download historical satellite images from the past 40 years.
  2. Image Processing: The script processes the retrieved images, generating timelapse visuals that highlight the changes in the water body over time.

Future Development Plans

The next steps to further enhance the project include:

  1. Web Application Devlopment and Deployment: Transform the existing Python script into a user-friendly web application that allows users to easily interact with the tool by simply entering the coordinates of a location.
  2. Water Level Analysis: Implement advanced image processing algorithms to measure and track changes in water levels year over year, plotting these changes in an interactive graph.
  3. Forecasting with Machine Learning: Design and develop a deep learning model capable of predicting future water levels based on historical data. The model will issue alerts if the forecasted levels indicate a critical decline.
